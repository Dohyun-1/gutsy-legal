Consumer Health Data Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 16, 2026

This policy describes how Gutsy collects, uses, shares, and protects consumer health data, as required by the Washington My Health My Data Act and similar laws. It applies in addition to our general Privacy Policy.

Categories of consumer health data we collect

  • The health condition you choose to tailor scores to (Crohn's disease, or type 2 diabetes / prediabetes).
  • Crohn's disease severity and current disease phase (e.g., remission or flare) that you enter.
  • The diabetes type you select (type 2, prediabetes, or not sure).
  • Food allergies and food sensitivities you enter.
  • Health-related answers from the onboarding questionnaire.
  • Your food scan history, to the extent it reflects dietary management of your condition.
  • Label photos you submit, which are used once for analysis and then deleted.
  • The Monthly Report we generate when you open it, which summarizes that month's scan activity through the lens of your condition, and which we save so it does not have to be rewritten each time.

Why we collect it

We collect this data only to provide the app's core function you request: scoring packaged foods against the settings you enter, and saving your history and preferences. We do not use consumer health data for advertising, and we do not use it for any purpose unrelated to providing the app.

Sources of the data

Consumer health data comes directly from you — what you type into the app and the label photos you choose to submit. The one exception is the Monthly Report, which we generate for you from the scan history you created. We do not obtain consumer health data from any other source.

How and with whom we share it

We do not sell consumer health data, and we do not share it for advertising. We share it only with the service providers needed to operate the app:

  • Supabase, Inc. — the database and authentication provider that securely stores your account and the health data you enter.
  • OpenAI — receives two things, each only to produce a result you asked for. First, a label photo you submit, used once to read the product's text and then deleted. Second, when you open your Monthly Report: the condition your scores are tailored to, together with that month's scan count, average score, and score breakdown. It never receives your name, email, account identifier, severity, allergies, or the individual products you scanned.

Apple and Google process only sign-in data, not your health data.

Your rights

Under the Washington My Health My Data Act (and similar laws) you may:

  • Confirm and access the consumer health data we hold about you.
  • Withdraw consent to our collection and sharing.
  • Delete your consumer health data.

To access or delete your data, use "Delete my data" / "Delete account" in the app, or email rdh2993@gmail.com. To withdraw the optional sharing consent, use Settings → Consent & data sharing. We will honor verified requests as required by law and will not discriminate against you for exercising these rights.

Data retention and security

We keep consumer health data only while your account is active; label photos are deleted right after analysis. Data is encrypted in transit and at rest.
